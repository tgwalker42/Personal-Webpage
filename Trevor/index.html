Current Thesis Research

Effects of Aquatic Insect Biomass on Bat Species Diversity

As a Master's Student at Austin Peay State University I became very interested in ecological trophic level interactions and how these interactions could be used to infer information from the surrounding environment. Past research has linked aquatic insect larva such as MAyflies, Stoneflies, and Caddisflies to assess stream health, and other research has correlated bat diversity/abundance to the adult forms of these insects via biomass, but none has explored the relationship amongst all three. So I am looking at community structures at all three levels (1. Aquatic Mayflies, Stoneflies, and Caddisfly Larva, 2. Aquatic Insect Adults, 3. Bat Species Diversity Metrics) to search for correlatons of bat species abundance and evenness. What I expect to find is significant correlations with certain species of bats, and if that is found it could be suggested that species of bat could also be used as a bioindicator of stream health.
